## Literature Survey on Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) for Weight Management in Non-Diabetic Patients

### Key Findings

1. **Efficacy of Semaglutide for Weight Management**:
   - Semaglutide, marketed as Wegovy, is specifically approved for weight management in non-diabetic patients. It has been shown to be effective in promoting significant weight loss in this population [1][3].
   - A systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials (RCTs) demonstrated that semaglutide significantly reduces body weight in obese or overweight patients without diabetes [3].

2. **Safety Considerations**:
   - The safety profile of semaglutide includes common side effects such as gastrointestinal issues, which are more pronounced at higher doses used for weight management compared to doses used for diabetes management [2][4].
   - Long-term safety data is still being evaluated, but current studies suggest that semaglutide is generally well-tolerated [5].

3. **Comparison with Other Treatments**:
   - Wegovy, which contains semaglutide, is administered at higher doses than Ozempic, leading to greater weight loss but also a higher likelihood of side effects [2].
   - The choice between Wegovy and other semaglutide formulations depends on individual patient factors, including the presence of diabetes and the desired weight loss outcome [2].

4. **Research Gaps and Future Directions**:
   - While current studies support the efficacy of semaglutide for weight loss in non-diabetic patients, further research is needed to understand the long-term effects and safety of sustained use [5].
   - Additional studies could explore the comparative effectiveness of semaglutide against other weight management interventions.
